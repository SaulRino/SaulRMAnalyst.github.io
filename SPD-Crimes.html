Summary

Goal

The goal was to identify crime trends and areas with consistently high crime rates in Seattle, to inform strategies for crime reduction and improve community safety.

Process

- Data Cleaning & Transformation: Used Python to map neighborhood names to zip codes, split multiple zip codes, update latitude/longitude values, and remove duplicates.

- Exporting Data: The cleaned dataset was exported into CSV files for further analysis.

- SQL Data Analysis: Applied Common Table Expressions (CTEs) to filter and count violent and property crimes.

- Grouping & Key Generation: Created tables to group data by neighborhood, zip code, and precinct; assigned unique identifiers to offenses and neighborhoods

Insights

- The sharp spike in offenses around 2020, with a peak of 767 on a single day, likely correlates to the Covid-19 pandemic, Scammers exploited the pandemic by stealing personal information (Identity Theft) to apply for unemployment and small business aid

- The table shows that Capitol Hill has the highest number of offenses in the MCPP, with 21,951 crimes, followed by Downtown Commercial (19,944) and Queen Anne (19,680). These neighborhoods experience notably higher crime rates, suggesting a need for targeted policing or community safety initiatives

- The time of day in which crime occurres more often is at night by using historical data from 08'-present

- The offense that has occurred the most in the past 5 years in Seattle has been Burglary, Theft from Motor Vehicle and Vandalism according to the MCPP High Offenses graph
