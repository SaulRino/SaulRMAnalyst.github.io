Summary

Goal

The goal of this project was to analyze Super Bowl outcomes to uncover key patterns. I addressed two questions: (1) What percentage of Super Bowls were decided by a margin of 10 points or less versus more than 10 points? (2) Which team has the most Super Bowl wins, and what is their average winning margin? Additionally, I categorized games as "close" or "blowout" based on the scoring margin to evaluate the competitiveness of Super Bowl games

Process

- Ensured completeness of key columns (Winner Pts and Loser Pts)

- Addressed null values to avoid skewing results

- Added Winning Margin (Winner Pts - Loser Pts) to assess victory sizes

- Introduced Losing Margin for comprehensive comparisons

Insights

- Scoring Patterns Over Time: Earlier Super Bowls saw lower average scores, reflecting stronger defenses. Recent decades show a rise in offensive dominance and higher-scoring games

- Consistency of Powerhouse Teams: Teams like the Steelers and Patriots maintained dominance across multiple eras, showcasing sustained excellence

- Close Game Trends: Close games became slightly more frequent after the 1990s, indicating a growing parity among teams

- Team Performance: Most Super Bowl wins: Pittsburgh Steelers (6) and New England Patriots (6). Steelers’ average winning margin: 7.5 points (higher than Patriots’ 4.83), reflecting greater dominance
